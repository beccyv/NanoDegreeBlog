When Did Cruising Get So Cool?

Once the poor relation of the holiday world and loved only by those of retirement age, cruising has undergone a massive image transformation in recent years. Newer cruise lines such as Virgin Cruises have finally managed to rehabilitate the reputation of a holiday on the seas - and the idea of seeing the world, whilst packing once, has really taken hold.

These days, there's a cruise for everyone. Whether you are a family with children seeking the thrills and spills of the slides, skating and movies on Royal Caribbean or a bright young thing who loves to party on one of the new adult only ships, there's a cruise you'll love.

Emma Godwin, a young mother in her thirties, describes her realisation that cruising might suit her family:

"To be honest, I thought cruising was just for the elderly, but then a few of my friends who are parents started taking Disney cruises and it just progressed from there. When you have children it's just such an easy way to travel. Having said that, my niece in her twenties goees on party cruises now too, so I guess it's not just for families either."

How to find the right cruise for you?

When it comes to picking the right cruise, the most important thing is to work out what you want from a holiday. If yours is a child focused getaway pick one of the family friendly cruise lines. With ships filled with bowling alleys, death defying slides and even ice rinks, there really isn't much you can't do aboard a family friendly cruise ship - for a price. If you prefer something a little more about the nightlife and a little less about the little people, be sure to find one of the cruise lines that is adults only and focuses on evening entertainment and get ready to party the night away.

By contrast, if you prefer a holiday that's a little more sedate, look out for cruise lines that operate at a somewhat slower pace. If you don't like throngs of people and non stop entertainment, but would rather while away the days with a book, there are cruise lines that focus on the food and ambiance and offer a gentle wander around the high seas.

As with every holiday experience it's best to go armed with knowledge when you book. We suggest reading Cruise Critic reviews to learn more about any ship or line you are considering booking. Best of all, talk to friends who have cruised and enjoy the same types of holidays as you do. Youtube has a wealth of information about cruising and walkthroughs of every ship so you can see what you are getitng before you book.

Of course, a great travel agent can also be invaluable. There's nothing better than someone who knows the world of cruising and can help you book the trip of your dreams.
